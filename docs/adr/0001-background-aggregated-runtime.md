# Background Aggregated Runtime

`1mcp serve --background` starts a persistent **Background Runtime Supervisor** for the selected **Runtime Scope**. The supervisor owns exactly one **Background Aggregated Runtime**, waits for it to become ready, and replaces it after an unexpected exit. `1mcp serve --status`, `1mcp serve --stop`, and `1mcp serve --restart` own lifecycle inspection, deliberate shutdown, and recovery from terminal failure. Client surfaces such as `proxy`, `inspect`, and `run` remain attach-only rather than implicitly starting or stopping runtimes.

Runtime uniqueness is scoped to the configuration directory, not the machine. Ownership must be race-safe: an ordinary foreground or background `serve` command exits non-zero whenever its **Runtime Scope** is already owned, while an explicit alternate `--config-dir` may run its own runtime. Foreground HTTP and deprecated stdio runtimes participate in scoped ownership but remain unsupervised; background supervision remains HTTP-only because stdio uses the invoking process stdin/stdout. Removing stdio is a separate breaking change, and users should migrate to `1mcp proxy`.

The supervisor treats every worker exit not initiated by `serve --stop` or `serve --restart` as a crash, including exits before readiness. It retries at most five times, after delays of 1, 2, 4, 8, and 16 seconds. The counter resets only after a replacement reaches readiness and remains alive for five minutes. A live but unhealthy worker is reported as unreachable and is not killed automatically. After exhausting the limit, the supervisor remains resident in `crash-loop` state without a worker until an explicit stop or restart; restart replaces the supervisor and worker and resets the counter.

Lifecycle operations fail closed. If the supervisor disappears while its worker remains alive, the scope is `orphaned`: ordinary starts still fail, while stop or restart performs recovery. Status preserves the existing exit codes and adds `5` for `restarting`, `6` for `crash-loop`, and `7` for `orphaned`. Replacement workers reuse the original effective launch configuration, and supervisor events append to the scope's background log. Automatic startup after a machine reboot and health-based recovery are out of scope.
